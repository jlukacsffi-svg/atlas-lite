# Atlas Capital Research Privacy Policy

Status: Draft for legal review. Not effective and not approved for external
users.

Draft date: June 13, 2026

## Scope

This draft describes the planned privacy practices for an invite-only Atlas
Capital Research service. Atlas is currently an owner-only research system.
No public registration or external customer service is active.

## Information Atlas May Collect

- Account data: name, email address, identity-provider subject, tenant,
  membership, role, account status, and authentication/security events.
- User content: watchlists, portfolios, paper accounts, research tasks,
  reports, notes, preferences, and support communications.
- Financial context supplied by the user: holdings, cost basis, simulated
  transactions, and benchmark preferences. Atlas does not currently connect to
  a brokerage or execute real trades.
- Technical data: IP address, device and browser information, timestamps,
  session identifiers, request logs, error logs, and security telemetry.
- Product records: invitations, access changes, audit events, privacy requests,
  exports, and deletion status.

Atlas should not request brokerage passwords, bank credentials, Social
Security numbers, government identification, or payment-card data during the
invite-only research stage.

## How Information May Be Used

- Authenticate users and enforce tenant isolation.
- Provide research, portfolio analysis, reports, alerts, and paper trading.
- Operate, secure, troubleshoot, back up, and improve the service.
- Detect abuse, investigate incidents, and preserve audit integrity.
- Respond to support, export, correction, and deletion requests.
- Meet legal, regulatory, licensing, and recordkeeping obligations.

Atlas must not sell personal information or use private portfolio data for
third-party advertising.

## Service Providers And Disclosures

Planned providers include Google Cloud for hosting, storage, identity, logs,
and security operations. Other vendors may be added only after privacy,
security, contract, and cost review.

Information may be disclosed:

- To service providers acting for Atlas under appropriate restrictions.
- At the user's direction.
- To investigate abuse or protect users, Atlas, or others.
- When required by law or valid legal process.
- In a business transaction, subject to notice and appropriate safeguards.

Atlas must not expose one tenant's private data to another tenant.

## Retention

Atlas follows `DATA_RETENTION_SCHEDULE.md`. Retention depends on record type,
account status, security needs, tenant ownership, legal requirements, backup
cycles, and unresolved disputes. Deleted identities may be pseudonymized while
tenant-owned research and append-only security records are retained.

## Privacy Choices

The planned service supports:

- Access to account and tenant information.
- Correction of inaccurate account information.
- Tenant-scoped data export.
- Non-owner account deletion requests and cancellation.
- Owner-confirmed deletion completion.
- Session revocation and account disabling.

Requests will be verified. Some information may be retained for security,
fraud prevention, legal obligations, dispute resolution, tenant ownership, or
backup rotation. Atlas will explain material exceptions.

## Security

Planned safeguards include managed authentication, privileged-account MFA,
short-lived sessions, least privilege, encryption in transit and at rest,
tenant-scoped authorization, append-only audit events, monitoring, backups,
restore drills, and incident response. No system can guarantee absolute
security.

## Children

Atlas is intended for adults and is not directed to children under 18. Public
or self-service registration must remain disabled until age and consent
requirements are reviewed.

## International And State-Specific Rights

Atlas has not completed a jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction applicability review.
The product is designed to support access, correction, deletion, export, and
limited retention, but legal counsel must determine required notices,
verification methods, response periods, appeals, and exceptions before an
external beta.

## Changes

Material changes should receive a new effective date and appropriate notice.
Historical versions should remain available.
